# The Architecture of Silence

**A Literary Non-Fiction Book by Aaron King**

---

## About

A family that fled persecution built the licensing frameworks of England, then enforced the colonial order in Australia, then found themselves inside the architecture they had created - until one of them finally named it.

This book traces a single thread from the St Bartholomew's Day Massacre in 1572, through the founding of the Society of Apothecaries, through colonial Australia, through the Constitution and the Dismissal, to a Navy Electronics Technician standing on a fishing boat at Ashmore Reef asking a simple question: *Why is there no licence?*

## The Central Tension

- **THE HAND** - Enforcing the system, serving power, inside the architecture
- **NOT THE HAND** - Subject to the system, outside power, inside the architecture as victim
- **NAMING IT** - The first to write it down
