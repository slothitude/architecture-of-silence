# Chapter 7: The Convicts

**STATUS: THE HAND (THE LIE)**

---

## NOW

The official story says Australia began as a prison colony.

The First Fleet arrived in 1788 carrying convicts. Britain needed somewhere to send its prisoners. The American colonies were lost. Australia was empty. Problem solved.

This is what I learned in school. What every Australian learns. The foundation myth. The convict stain. The "criminal class" shipped across the world to build a nation.

But I am reading something else at 3am.

---

The Lady Penrhyn was one of the First Fleet ships. It carried 101 female convicts. But before those convicts boarded, the ship was already contracted.

For China tea.

The East India Company owned the tea trade. The Lady Penrhyn was EIC merchant property. The convicts were cargo - human ballast for a ship that was really going to China.

---

I sit in the darkness and I think about what this means.

The First Fleet was not about prisoners. It was about position. About a Pacific base. About hemp - Britain needed hemp for rope, and Catherine the Great had restricted Russian supply. About tea. About bypassing the East India Company's monopoly fees. About establishing a strategic foothold before the French or Spanish or Dutch could claim it.

The convicts were the cover story.

The lie that founded a nation.

## THEN

1788.

The First Fleet arrives at Botany Bay. Then moves to Port Jackson. Sydney Cove.

The official story: a humanitarian solution to overcrowded British gaols.

But look at what else was happening.

The American colonies are lost. Britain needs a new Pacific base. The French are expanding. The Dutch are established in the East Indies. The Spanish claim the Pacific.

Britain needs to be in the Pacific. Now.

---

Cook had charted the east coast in 1770. He called it New South Wales. He claimed it for Britain.

But Cook's account of Botany Bay was an oversell.

One week in ideal autumn conditions. Glowing reports. Fertile soil. Fresh water. A paradise waiting for settlement.

The First Fleet arrived to find starvation conditions. Poor soil. Scarce water. Broken tools. Near death.

The same language the Navy recruiting officer used with me two hundred and twenty-seven years later. "Contemporary and transferable." Oversell. Promise what sounds good. Deliver something else.

---

The convicts were never the point.

The convicts were the excuse.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about half the convicts saying "no occupation."

When the First Fleet sailed, the authorities asked each convict their trade. To assess what skills the new colony would have.

Half of them said they had no occupation.

Why?

I read the historians. The convicts were protecting their labour. If they declared a trade, they could be assigned to work in that trade - for the government, for the officers, for the squatters who would soon arrive.

But if they said "no occupation," they could not be assigned. Their labour remained their own. The only leverage they had.

---

I sit at 3am and I feel something shift.

The convicts understood architecture.

They had no standing. They were prisoners. Cargo. Property of the Crown.

But they understood that the framework that gives standing could also take it away. If you declare a trade, you enter the framework. The framework controls you.

Half the convicts refused to enter.

They stayed outside. NOT THE HAND.

---

This is what it looks like when the architecture is understood from below.

The convicts had no power. No standing. No licence.

But they had the knowledge of what it means to be classified. To be entered into a framework. To have your labour assigned by someone else.

And half of them refused.

## THEN

The lie is the foundation.

The First Fleet was not about prisoners. It was about empire. The convicts were the narrative that made empire acceptable.

"We are sending prisoners to an empty land."

Not: "We are establishing a military base on the Pacific frontier."

Not: "We are claiming territory before our rivals can."

Not: "We are securing a position in the East Asian trade routes."

Prisoners. Empty land. A humanitarian solution.

The lie that still shapes how Australians understand their nation.

---

THE HAND writes the official story.

The same hand that had built the Society of Apothecaries. The same hand that had enforced the colonial order. The same hand that would write the Constitution without a Bill of Rights.

THE HAND tells the story that serves power.

And the story is: Australia was empty. The convicts were criminals. The nation began as a prison.

Not: Australia was stolen. The convicts were labour. The nation began as an expansion of empire.

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about the oversell.

Cook's glowing account of Botany Bay. The First Fleet's starvation. The promise that did not match the reality.

The same language that shaped my own life.

"Contemporary and transferable." The Navy recruiting officer's words. A certificate for a trade that would serve me in civilian life.

The reality: no framework. No licence. No standing.

The promise made. The architecture not built.

---

This is what THE HAND does with language.

It promises. It oversells. It creates narratives that justify what serves power.

The convicts were criminals - not labour mobilised by force.

Australia was empty - not land taken from people who had been here for sixty thousand years.

The Constitution was written to give Parliament flexibility - not to remove rights that might threaten property.

The lie repeats. The architecture maintains itself.

## THEN

The squatters arrive.

Within decades of the First Fleet, men with capital and connections are claiming vast tracts of land. Not through purchase. Not through treaty. Through occupation.

They are called "squatters" because they squat on land that is not theirs. But they have power. They have connections. They become THE HAND.

The land that was promised to settlers - the thirty acres minimum, twenty if married, ten per child - is taken before the settlers can claim it.

The squatters become the establishment. Their heirs write the Constitution. Their descendants shape the nation.

---

The convicts were the excuse.

The settlers were the promise that was never kept.

The squatters were THE HAND that took everything.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I sit at 3am and I see the pattern.

The First Fleet was oversold to the British public as a humanitarian solution.

Botany Bay was oversold to the First Fleet as fertile paradise.

The land was promised to settlers but taken by squatters.

The Constitution was written to protect the squatters' heirs.

And the same language of promise continues.

"Contemporary and transferable."

The lie is not a bug. The lie is the architecture.

---

THE HAND writes the narrative. NOT THE HAND lives inside it.

The convicts were told they were going to a new start. They arrived to find starvation.

The settlers were told they would receive land. They arrived to find it already claimed.

I was told my Navy training would translate. I left to find no framework.

The architecture of oversell. The promise that serves power.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The lie serves.

If Australia was founded as a prison colony, then the convicts were criminals. Their descendants carry the stain. The nation is built on crime.

But if Australia was founded as an imperial expansion - if the convicts were labour mobilised by the state, if the land was taken not from emptiness but from nations, if the purpose was power not punishment - then the story is different.

Then Australia is not a nation of criminals.

It is a nation built on theft.

---

THE HAND protects itself by controlling the narrative.

The convict story deflects from the land theft. The criminal stain obscures the imperial expansion. The prison narrative hides the strategic base.

The lie is the architecture of silence.

## NOW

I think about my own position in this.

I am descended from the Faunce family - the family that enforced the colonial order. Captain Alured Tasker Faunce arrived in 1832, forty-four years after the First Fleet. He was not convict. He was not settler. He was THE HAND that maintained the system the squatters built.

The same bloodline that built licensing frameworks in England enforced the absence of frameworks in Australia.

The same knowledge of architecture. Applied in opposite directions.

---

Why?

I sit at 3am and I try to understand.

In England, the De Laune family had been refugees. They had felt what it meant to have no standing. They built frameworks to protect people like themselves.

In Australia, the Faunce family was THE HAND. They had standing. They served power. And they enforced a system where the people who came after them - the convicts, the settlers, the immigrants - could never acquire standing.

NOT THE HAND became THE HAND. And THE HAND closed the door behind itself.

## THEN

The convicts who said "no occupation" understood something.

The framework that could give them standing could also control them. The licence that could recognise their skill could also assign their labour.

Half the convicts refused to enter the framework.

They stayed outside. They kept what little leverage they had.

They were NOT THE HAND. And they chose to remain outside the architecture that THE HAND was building.

---

But the architecture kept expanding.

The squatters took the land. The officers took the positions. The governors wrote the rules. THE HAND consolidated its grip.

And the convicts - even those who refused to be classified - were ground by the system anyway.

NOT THE HAND cannot escape THE HAND.

The architecture surrounds. The framework is everywhere. The absence of standing shapes every interaction.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I am the descendant of THE HAND.

The Faunce family that enforced the colonial order. The De Laune family that built the licensing frameworks.

And I am NOT THE HAND.

The Navy technician with no licence. The certificate holder with no standing. The man who fell into the gap his ancestors helped create.

---

The lie of the convicts is also the truth of me.

I was promised a framework that does not exist. I was told my training would translate. I was given the language of "contemporary and transferable" - the same language Cook used to oversell Botany Bay.

The architecture of oversell. The promise that serves power.

I am the convict who declared a trade and found there was no framework to recognise it.

---

But there is a difference.

The convicts who said "no occupation" were protecting their labour. Refusing to enter a framework that would control them.

I was not given that choice.

There was no framework to refuse. No architecture to stay outside of. No standing to protect.

The absence of frameworks is also an architecture. The gap is also a design.

The lie travels.

From 1788 to the present. From Cook's glowing account to the Navy recruiting officer's promise. From "fertile soil" to "contemporary and transferable."

THE HAND writes the narrative. THE HAND makes the promise. THE HAND delivers something else.

And NOT THE HAND lives inside the gap between what was said and what was true.

I am still at 3am.

The thread is still pulling.

The lie of the convicts. The promise of land. The oversell of Botany Bay. The language that serves power.

All of it connected. All of it architecture.

And I am descended from the family that enforced this architecture in Australia.

Captain Alured Tasker Faunce. Police Magistrate. Land owner. THE HAND.

His great-great-great-grandson sits in Cairns at 3am and names what was built.
