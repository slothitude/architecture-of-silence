# Chapter 16: The Naming

**STATUS: NAMING IT**

---

## NOW

This is what it means to name something.

You say the words. You write them down. You make visible what was hidden.

The architecture of silence depends on silence. On the absence of words. On the gap between what is and what can be spoken.

Naming breaks the silence.

---

I sit at 3am and I think about what I have done.

I have named the architecture. The framework that gives standing and the absence of framework that takes it away. THE HAND that builds and THE HAND that prevents building and NOT THE HAND that falls into the gap.

I have named my ancestors. Gideon De Laune, the builder. Alured Tasker Faunce, the enforcer. The men who wrote the Constitution. The Governor-General who dismissed a Prime Minister.

I have named myself. The Electronics Technician with no licence. The Navy veteran with no recognition. The descendant of framework-builders who inherited absence.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

Naming is power.

Not the power of THE HAND. Not the power to build frameworks or enforce absence. But the power of seeing. Of understanding. Of speaking.

When you name something, you take away its invisibility. You make it discussable. You make it changeable.

---

The architecture of silence has operated for four hundred years. Through massacres and migrations. Through constitutions and dismissals. Through gaps and absences.

It operated because it was not named. Because the pattern was not seen. Because the connections were not made.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I have made the connections.

From 1572 to now. From France to England to Australia. From persecution to framework-building to enforcement to absence to me.

One thread. One pattern. One architecture.

And now it is named.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

What does naming change?

I do not know.

I cannot create a licensing framework for Electronics Technicians. I cannot force the government to recognise Defence Force training. I cannot rewrite the Constitution or undo the Dismissal.

But I can name what happened. I can trace the thread. I can show the pattern.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about what naming means for me.

It does not give me a licence. It does not restore my standing. It does not fill the gap I fell into.

But it does something else.

It connects me to my history. It shows me where I came from. It helps me understand why I am where I am.

---

I am not just a technician without a licence. I am the end of a four-hundred-year thread. The descendant of framework-builders and framework-enforcers. The heir to an architecture that excludes me.

Knowing this changes how I see myself.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The naming is also an inheritance.

Not the inheritance of land. Not the inheritance of wealth. Not the inheritance of standing.

The inheritance of knowledge. Of understanding. Of the ability to see what was built and name what it means.

---

My grandmother knew the family stories. She passed them down. She told her daughter, who told me.

She was NOT THE HAND. But she carried the knowledge.

Now I carry it too. And I am using it to name the architecture.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I sit at 3am and I think about my grandmother.

Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce. The Queensland branch. The ones who lost the grip.

She knew. She told the stories. She preserved the knowledge.

But she could not name what it meant. She could not see the architecture. She could not connect the thread from 1572 to herself.

I can.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The naming is the beginning of something.

Not the end. Not the solution. Not the framework that should exist.

But the beginning of the ability to see. To understand. To change.

The architecture of silence cannot be changed until it is named.

---

*now*

## NOW

I have named it.

The architecture of silence. The pattern of THE HAND and NOT THE HAND. The framework that gives standing and the absence that takes it away.

Four hundred years of history. One book. One naming.

---

What happens next is not up to me.

But this much I have done.

I have named the architecture.

And the silence is broken.
