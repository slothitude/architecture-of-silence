# Chapter 6: The Soldier

**STATUS: THE HAND**

---

## NOW

The military thread runs through the Faunce family like steel wire.

Major Thomas Faunce at Quebec, 1759. Major-General Alured Dodsworth Faunce in the Napoleonic Wars. Captain Alured Tasker Faunce in the 4th Regiment, arriving in Australia in 1832.

And then, generations later, me. Electronics Technician. Royal Australian Navy. 2004-2015.

The same family. The same pattern. Service to the Crown.

But different positions within the architecture.

---

I sit at 3am and I think about what it means to serve.

The Faunce men served as officers. They commanded. They held authority. They were THE HAND that carried the sword.

I served as a technician. I maintained systems. I followed orders. I was NOT THE HAND - or if I was, I did not know it yet.

The difference is standing.

An officer holds standing by virtue of commission. A technician holds standing only if there is a framework to recognise the trade.

There was no framework for me.

But there had been frameworks for my ancestors.

## THEN

1759. The Plains of Abraham. Quebec.

Major Thomas Faunce serves under General James Wolfe.

The battle lasts fifteen minutes. The British scale the cliffs, form lines, fire two volleys, charge. The French break. Both Wolfe and the French commander Montcalm die of wounds.

Canada becomes British.

---

Major Thomas Faunce is wounded in the battle. He survives. He becomes town major of Quebec during the occupation.

The hand holds the sword. The hand enforces the new order. The hand consolidates British power in North America.

This is what THE HAND does when it serves empire.

It expands. It conquers. It establishes control where none existed before.

---

The family that would one day marry into the De Laune framework-builders is already building something of its own. Not licensing frameworks - military frameworks. The architecture of force.

Gideon De Laune built standing through professional recognition. Thomas Faunce built standing through military service.

Two streams of THE HAND. Converging.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about conquest.

Thomas Faunce fought at Quebec. The British took Canada from the French. The architecture of empire expanded.

Four hundred years earlier, the De Laune family had fled France. Now their future in-laws were helping conquer French territory.

NOT THE HAND had become THE HAND. And THE HAND was eating its own.

---

The pattern repeats everywhere.

The persecuted become the persecutors. The refugees become the colonisers. The ones who fled across the Channel become the ones who cross oceans to take what others have.

Is this inevitable?

I sit at 3am and I cannot answer.

What I know is this: the Faunce bloodline that I carry participated in empire. Not as victims. Not as outsiders. As agents.

THE HAND holds the sword.

And I am descended from that hand.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The Napoleonic Wars. 1803-1815.

Major-General Alured Dodsworth Faunce serves in the war against France.

The same France that the De Launes fled in 1572. The same country that had killed seventy thousand Protestants in the St Bartholomew's Day Massacre. The same nation that had given the family its original trauma.

Now a Faunce fights against France.

---

What did Alured Dodsworth Faunce think about this?

We do not know. The records show his service. His rank. His survival.

But not his thoughts.

Did he know that his future wife's family had fled France? Did he know that the name he would pass to his children - Alured Dodsworth Faunce - echoed the French Protestant refugees who had crossed the Channel two hundred and fifty years before?

Or was it just war? Just service? Just the Crown's enemies, whoever they might be?

---

The Faunce family drops "De Laune" during this period.

Not formally. Not deliberately documented. But the name recedes. Faunce becomes the family identifier. De Laune fades.

France is the enemy. A French name is inconvenient.

The wound closes over. The name becomes English.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about names in wartime.

The De Laune family had been in England for over two hundred years by the Napoleonic Wars. They were English. They served the Crown.

But the name was still French.

And France was the enemy.

How many families changed their names during those years? How many German names became English during World War I? How many Japanese names disappeared during World War II?

The architecture of identity. The pressure to belong. The cost of standing out.

The De Laune name did not disappear. But it faded. And in 1864, Chapman Faunce would add it back - formally, deliberately, as if reclaiming something that had been set aside.

---

I sit at 3am and I think about my own name.

King.

It is English. Generic. The kind of name that tells you nothing about where someone came from.

My father's name. Not my mother's. Not Faunce. Not Stevens. Not De Laune.

The bloodlines I carry are invisible in my name.

But they are visible in the architecture I navigate. In the absence of frameworks. In the gaps that my ancestors helped create.

---

*then*

## THEN

The military tradition continues.

Major Thomas Faunce (wounded at Quebec, 1759) passes the tradition to his descendants.

Major-General Alured Dodsworth Faunce (Napoleonic Wars) passes it further.

Captain Alured Tasker Faunce (4th Regiment, Australia 1832) carries it to the other side of the world.

The hand that holds the sword travels. From England to Canada to Australia.

Empire expands. The architecture follows.

---

What is the relationship between military service and framework-building?

Gideon De Laune built frameworks that gave standing to medical practitioners. The Faunce family carried swords that enforced standing through force.

Two different methods. Same result.

THE HAND maintains power. Through licensing or through violence. Through professional recognition or through military occupation.

The architecture of standing can be built with pen or with sword.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I served in the Navy. Not as an officer. As a technician.

Eleven years. Six years as an Electronics Technician. Then the rest rebuilding, retraining, finding a place in a system that did not recognise my trade.

I never fired a weapon in anger. I never conquered territory. I never enforced occupation.

I maintained systems. I followed protocols. I did the work.

And when I left, I had no standing.

---

The Faunce men who served as officers - Thomas at Quebec, Alured in the Napoleonic Wars, Alured Tasker in colonial Australia - they left the military with standing. With land grants. With positions. With connections.

I left with a certificate for a trade that does not exist.

Same service. Different architecture.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The Faunce military line leads to Australia.

Captain Alured Tasker Faunce, born 1808, enters the 4th Regiment (The King's Own) at sixteen. By 1832, he is in Australia.

Why Australia?

The 4th Regiment is assigned to New South Wales. Garrison duty. Convict escort. The maintenance of colonial order.

The hand that held the sword at Quebec now holds it in Sydney.

---

Empire moves through families.

The De Launes fled persecution. The Faunces served the Crown. Together - through marriage - they produced a line that would enforce the colonial order in Australia.

NOT THE HAND became THE HAND.

THE HAND crossed the world.

And in Australia, THE HAND would enforce the absence of the very frameworks it had built in England.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I sit at 3am and I try to understand the transition.

Gideon De Laune built licensing frameworks in England.

His descendants - through the Faunce marriage - enforced the absence of frameworks in Australia.

The same bloodline. The same knowledge of how architecture works.

Applied in opposite directions.

---

What happened?

Did the family forget what Gideon had built? Did they not understand the connection between licensing frameworks and standing?

Or did they understand perfectly - and choose to maintain the gap?

THE HAND protects itself. THE HAND preserves its advantage. THE HAND ensures that NOT THE HAND cannot rise.

Did my ancestors build the architecture of silence deliberately?

---

I do not know.

But I am descended from them.

And I am the first one to name what they built.
