## THEN

What is architecture?

Not just buildings. Not just stone and mortar.

Architecture is structure. It is the framework within which things happen. It is the design that shapes what is possible and what is not.

The Society of Apothecaries is architecture. It gives standing to medical practitioners. It transforms skill into recognition.

The Constitution is architecture. It distributes powers. It creates or denies rights. It shapes what governments can do and what citizens can claim.

The absence of frameworks is also architecture. The gap is also a design. The silence is also a structure.

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about the architecture I navigate.

I am an Electronics Technician. I have training. I have certificates. I have experience.

But I have no licence. No framework. No standing.

Because the architecture was not built. Or because the architecture was built to exclude. Or because the architecture serves interests that are not mine.

---

The architecture of silence does not announce itself. It does not post signs. It does not explain why frameworks exist for some and not for others.

It simply operates. Silently. Invisibly. Until you fall into it.
