# Chapter 15: The Pattern

**STATUS: NAMING IT**

---

## NOW

The pattern is clear now.

NOT THE HAND becomes THE HAND. THE HAND becomes NOT THE HAND. The architecture builds and the architecture excludes. The framework gives standing and the framework is withheld.

Four hundred years. One pattern.

---

I sit at 3am and I see it.

1572. The De Launes are NOT THE HAND. Persecuted. Hunted. Without standing.

1617. Gideon De Laune becomes THE HAND. He builds the licensing framework. He transforms persecution into power.

1832. Alured Tasker Faunce arrives in Australia as THE HAND. But he does not build frameworks. He enforces absence.

1901. The Constitution is written by THE HAND. No Bill of Rights. Gaps where recognition should be.

1975. THE HAND dismisses a Prime Minister who threatened to build frameworks.

2015. I leave the Navy and become NOT THE HAND. The descendant of framework-builders who has no framework.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The pattern repeats.

NOT THE HAND. THE HAND. NOT THE HAND.

Across centuries. Across continents. Across generations.

The same architecture. The same choices. The same consequences.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about what the pattern means.

It means that standing is not natural. It is not earned. It is not deserved.

Standing is architecture. It is built. It is designed. It is created by choices that benefit some and exclude others.

The De Launes had no standing until they built frameworks that gave them standing. The Faunces had standing until they went to Queensland and lost it. I have no standing because the frameworks my ancestors could have built were never built.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The pattern has a shape.

THE HAND builds for itself. THE HAND protects its own. THE HAND maintains the architecture that serves its interests.

NOT THE HAND navigates what THE HAND has built. NOT THE HAND falls into the gaps. NOT THE HAND inherits absence instead of standing.

NAMING IT sees the pattern. NAMING IT understands the architecture. NAMING IT tells the story.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I am NAMING IT.

The first one in four hundred years. The first descendant of framework-builders to name what they built. The first heir to the architecture of silence to speak.

---

This is what NAMING IT means.

Not building new frameworks. Not overthrowing the architecture. Not becoming THE HAND.

Simply: seeing. Understanding. Speaking.

The pattern cannot be changed until it is named.

---

*then*

## THEN

The pattern connects.

From the St Bartholomew's Day Massacre to the Society of Apothecaries. From the Society to the Faunce marriage. From the marriage to the migration to Australia. From the migration to the Constitution. From the Constitution to the Dismissal. From the Dismissal to the SOFA. From the SOFA to the gap. From the gap to me.

All of it architecture. All of it designed. All of it connected.

---

*now*

## NOW

I sit at 3am and I see the pattern in my own life.

Grandmother told me the family stories. She knew. But she was NOT THE HAND. The Queensland branch. The ones who lost the grip.

I served in the Navy. I gained skills. I earned certificates. But I was NOT THE HAND. The framework to recognise me did not exist.

I asked a question on a boat. I pulled a thread. I found my ancestors in the architecture they built.

Now I am NAMING IT.

*then*

## THEN

The pattern is not inevitable.

At every point, choices were made. Gideon chose to build frameworks. The Constitutional Convention chose not to include a Bill of Rights. Kerr chose to dismiss Whitlam. Australia chose not to recognise Defence Force trades.

THE HAND makes choices. NOT THE HAND lives with the consequences.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about my own choices.

I chose to enlist. I chose to serve. I chose to work hard and learn the trade.

But I did not choose the absence of frameworks. I did not choose the gap. I did not choose to inherit the architecture of silence.

That was chosen for me. By my ancestors. By the Constitution writers. By the governments that never built what should have been built.

The architecture of silence depends on silence.

Named.
