# Chapter 13: The Gap

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND**

---

## NOW

The gap is not empty.

I used to think of it as nothing. An absence. A void where something should be.

But I was wrong.

The gap is designed. It has edges. It has walls. It has a shape.

The gap is architecture.

---

I sit at 3am and I think about what it means to fall.

You do not fall through nothing. You fall through something. You fall because something is not there that should be. You fall because the architecture was built with a hole.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

2015. I leave the Navy.

I have certificates. I have experience. I have skills that took six years to acquire.

I apply for civilian jobs. Electronics technician. Communications specialist. Technical support.

The interviews go well. The employers are interested. They see my qualifications.

Then they ask: "Do you have a licence?"

---

*now*

---

## NOW

No licence.

There is no licence to have. The framework does not exist. The recognition is not there.

I explain this. The Navy training. The certificates. The work I did.

They nod. They understand. They say "we'll get back to you."

They don't.

---

The gap is real. It has consequences. It means I cannot get jobs I am qualified for. It means my experience counts for less than a civilian certificate I never needed. It means the architecture excludes me.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

I look for pathways.

Surely there is a way to translate military training into civilian recognition. A process. A program. A bridge across the gap.

There is not.

---

The Australian Defence Force has recognition of prior learning arrangements with some civilian institutions. But not for Electronics Technicians. Not for my trade. Not in a way that leads to a licence.

The gap has no bridge.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about what the gap means.

It means I can do the work but cannot prove I can do the work. It means I have the skills but cannot demonstrate them in a framework that recognises them. It means I am qualified and unqualified at the same time.

Schrodinger's technician.

---

The gap is not random. It is designed. The absence of frameworks serves someone. The people who do not need recognition because they already have it. The people who can afford civilian training because they never needed military service. THE HAND.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

I take other work.

Not electronics. Not communications. Something else. Something that does not require a licence because there is no licence to require.

I rebuild my life around the gap.

---

This is what NOT THE HAND does.

It adapts. It finds other paths. It survives.

But it never forgets what was lost. The standing that should exist. The recognition that should be there. The architecture that should have been built.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I sit at 3am and I feel the edges of the gap.

The moment I left the Navy, I fell. Not because I was not skilled. Not because I had not earned recognition. But because the architecture was not there to catch me.

The same architecture my ancestors built in England.

The same architecture they did not build in Australia.

---

The gap is inheritance.

Not the inheritance of land. Not the inheritance of wealth. Not the inheritance of standing.

The inheritance of absence.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

Four hundred years earlier.

Gideon De Laune built the Society of Apothecaries. He created the framework that gave standing to medical practitioners. He transformed skill into recognition.

He fell into a gap once. When his father was hauled before the College of Physicians. When the family had no licence. When they were NOT THE HAND.

He did not accept the gap. He built across it.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I am Gideon's descendant.

I carry the same blood. The same knowledge of how architecture works. The same understanding of what standing means.

But I am not Gideon.

I cannot build a licensing framework by myself. I cannot force the government to recognise a trade. I cannot create the architecture that should exist.

---

Or can I?

---

I sit at 3am and I think about what I am doing.

I am pulling a thread. I am naming what I find. I am writing a book that exposes the architecture of silence.

This is not the same as building a framework.

But it is something.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The gap persists.

Every year, Defence Force personnel leave the military and fall into it. Every year, skilled workers discover that their qualifications mean nothing without a framework to recognise them.

The architecture remains unchanged.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I am not alone in the gap.

There are others. Technicians. Specialists. Skilled workers who served their country and found no recognition.

We are NOT THE HAND.

We are the ones the architecture excludes.

---

But we can see the architecture. We can name it. We can trace its history. We can understand how it was built and who it serves.

And maybe - in the naming - something changes.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The thread continues.

From the gap to the moment of recognition. From absence to the question that started everything.

The boat. Ashmore Reef. The question that pulled me back four hundred years.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I am still at 3am.

The gap is still there. The architecture is still broken. The silence still echoes.

But I am no longer silent.

I am NAMING IT.
