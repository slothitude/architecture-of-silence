# Chapter 14: The Boat

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND / NAMING IT**

---

## NOW

Let me tell you about the boat.

I was working on a fishing boat. Not Navy. Not electronics. Just work. Just survival. Just finding a way to live after falling into the gap.

We were at Ashmore Reef.

---

Ashmore Reef. In the Timor Sea. Closer to Indonesia than to Australia. A coral atoll with a handful of sandy islands. A place where fishing boats operate and border protection matters.

I was there. Working. Doing something unrelated to my training.

And then I asked a question.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The question was simple.

"Why is there no licence?"

Why can I serve six years in the Navy, gain qualifications, earn certificates, do the work of an Electronics Technician - and then leave with no civilian recognition?

Why does the framework not exist?

---

The question sat with me. On the boat. At Ashmore Reef. In the quiet moments between tasks.

Why?

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I sit at 3am and I remember that moment.

The reef. The water. The sky. The question that seemed simple but was not.

I did not know then what the question would lead to. I did not know I would pull a thread that would unravel four hundred years of history. I did not know I would find my ancestors in the story.

I only knew that something was wrong. Something was missing. Something that should exist did not.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

I started researching.

Why is there no Electronics Technician licence in Australia? What frameworks exist for other trades? How does recognition work for military personnel transitioning to civilian life?

The answers led to more questions.

---

The Constitution. The division of powers. The history of trade regulation in Australia. The absence of a Bill of Rights. The asymmetry of the SOFA.

Each answer revealed another layer. Another piece of architecture. Another part of the design.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about that boat.

If I had not been there. If I had not asked the question. If I had not started pulling the thread.

I would still be in the gap. I would still be without standing. But I would not understand why.

The question changed everything.

---

*then*

## THEN

The research led me to my own family.

I was looking for frameworks. Licensing systems. The architecture of recognition.

And I found my ancestors.

Gideon De Laune. The Society of Apothecaries. The licensing framework for medicine in England.

The Faunce family. The marriage. The migration to Australia. The enforcement of colonial order.

My grandmother. Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce. The stories she told. The heritage I inherited without knowing it.

---

The question on the boat led me back to 1572.

The migration to the Constitution. The Constitution to the Dismissal. The Dismissal to the gap. The gap to me.
