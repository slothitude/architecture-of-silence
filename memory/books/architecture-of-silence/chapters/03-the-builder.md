# Chapter 3: The Builder

**STATUS: BECOMING THE HAND**

---

## NOW

3am, Cairns, 2024.

The screen glows in the dark room. I have been researching for hours. The thread I started pulling on that fishing boat at Ashmore Reef has led me here - to a name, a date, a charter.

Gideon De Laune. 1617. The Society of Apothecaries.

I read the words on the screen. My ancestor - the seven-year-old boy who fled the St Bartholomew's Day Massacre - grew up to become apothecary to the Queen of England. He worked with Francis Bacon. He helped found a society that still exists today. He built the licensing framework for medicine in England.

The family that had NO STANDING built the framework that GIVES standing.

NOT THE HAND became THE HAND.

I sit in the darkness and I try to understand what this means. My ancestor was hauled before the College of Physicians for practising without a licence. Twenty-five years later, his son helped create the framework that would licence medical practitioners across England and Wales.

The persecuted became the licencers.

And four hundred years later, I am standing in the same place William De Laune stood - without a licence, without a framework, without standing.

Except there is a difference.

William De Laune faced the College of Physicians. I face nothing. No college. No framework. No licensing body at all.

The framework Gideon built does not exist for Electronics Technicians.

The architecture his family helped create in England was never built in Australia.

## THEN

London, 1617.

The year the Rosicrucian manifestos are at their peak. Across Europe, four hundred manuscripts and books are being published discussing a secret society that may or may not exist. The Fama Fraternitatis. The Confessio Fraternitatis. The Chymical Wedding of Christian Rosenkreutz. Some call it revelation. Some call it hoax. Some call it the beginning of a new way of thinking about knowledge and power and the hidden structures of the world.

In London, Gideon De Laune is not thinking about Rosicrucians.

He is thinking about apothecaries.

---

The Worshipful Company of Grocers has controlled the spice and drug trade in London for centuries. Apothecaries - the men who compound medicines, who know the herbs and the chemicals and the remedies - are members of the Grocers' Company. They are subordinate to merchants who know nothing of medicine, who have never compounded a prescription, who cannot tell scammony from colocynth.

Gideon De Laune is an apothecary. He has been one since he was old enough to work in his father's shop. He learned the trade not through apprenticeship but through necessity - a refugee's son, building a skill that could not be taken away.

By 1610, he is apothecary to Anne of Denmark, Queen of James I.

This is not a small thing. To serve the Queen is to have access. To have access is to have influence. To have influence is to have the possibility of change.

---

Gideon is not alone.

There are other apothecaries in London who want to separate from the Grocers' Company. They want their own guild. Their own standards. Their own control over who can practice and who cannot.

The legal work preparing their case is said to be under the guidance of Francis Bacon.

Bacon is Lord Chancellor of England. He is also a philosopher, a scientist, a man who believes in the advancement of learning through systematic inquiry. He will approve the charter for the Society of Apothecaries himself.

Whether Bacon is Rosicrucian or not is debated. What matters is this: in 1617, at the height of the Rosicrucian furore, the most powerful intellectual in England helps create a society of apothecaries - men who deal in herbs and chemicals and the transformation of substances.

Alchemy by another name.

## NOW

I sit at 3am and I read about Francis Bacon.

The conspiracy theories are everywhere. Bacon wrote Shakespeare. Bacon founded the Rosicrucians. Bacon was the secret son of Elizabeth I. Bacon encoded messages in his works that reveal hidden truths about the world.

Most of this is nonsense. The Friedman cryptographers debunked the cipher claims in 1957. The Shakespeare authorship theories have been rejected by every serious scholar. The Rosicrucian leadership claims appeared two hundred and fifty years after Bacon's death.

But this is not what interests me.

What interests me is the timing.

1617. The peak of Rosicrucian discussion in Europe. The year the Society of Apothecaries is chartered. The year Gideon De Laune - my ancestor - helps build the licensing framework for medicine in England.

Whether Bacon was Rosicrucian does not matter.

What matters is that the Society of Apothecaries was created at a moment when secret knowledge, hidden structures, and the transformation of substances were being discussed across Europe.

And my ancestor was in the middle of it.

## THEN

6 December 1617.

King James I grants a royal charter to the Worshipful Society of Apothecaries.

One hundred and fourteen English apothecaries are named as founding members. Gideon De Laune is among them.

The charter gives the Society the power to control the practice of pharmacy in London. In 1815, the Apothecaries Act will extend that power across England and Wales. The Society will be given the statutory right to conduct examinations and grant licences to practice medicine.

The framework that William De Laune lacked - the standing that comes from recognition by a licensing body - will be built by his son.

---

Gideon serves the Society for decades. Junior Warden in 1624. Senior Warden in 1627. Master in 1628-29. Master again in 1637.

He is "noted for many singularities" - a phrase that will appear in the records, that historians will note without explaining. Singularities. Distinctive, unusual, remarkable characteristics. Something not quite ordinary about this man.

What were his singularities?

We do not know. The records do not say.

But we know this: Gideon De Laune arrived in England as a seven-year-old refugee with nothing. He died at ninety-four leaving an estate worth ninety-three thousand pounds. He owned property in three countries. His white marble statue stood in Apothecaries' Hall.

The refugee had become the establishment.

NOT THE HAND had become THE HAND.

## NOW

I think about what it means to build a framework.

Gideon De Laune did not overthrow the College of Physicians. He did not burn down the guild system. He did not rage against the architecture that had excluded his father.

He built something new within it.

The Society of Apothecaries gave standing to men who had none. It created a licensing framework that recognised training and skill. It transformed the ability to compound medicines from a craft practised at the mercy of merchants into a profession with its own standards and its own recognition.

This is how THE HAND is built.

Not through revolution. Through construction.

---

I sit at 3am and I think about my own situation.

I have no Electronics Technician licence because no framework exists. I cannot create a framework by myself. I cannot force the government to recognise a trade that it has chosen not to recognise.

But I can write.

I can pull the thread all the way back. I can find the places where the architecture was built. I can name what was done and who benefited and why the gaps exist.

I cannot build a licensing framework.

But I can name the architecture that ensures no framework exists.

And maybe - in the naming - something changes.

## THEN

Gideon De Laune builds more than a society.

He builds a fortune.

"De Laune's Pills" - a secret remedy for scurvy, jaundice, venereal disease, worms, dropsy. A formula that enters the pharmacopoeia as "Pilula ex Duobus" - the pill of two ingredients. Mainly colocynth and scammony, but prepared in a way that others cannot duplicate.

The pills make Gideon wealthy. At his death, his estate is worth ninety-three thousand pounds - an immense sum in 1659. He owns an estate at Roxton in Bedfordshire. He owns the manor of Sharsted in Kent. He owns a mansion in Blackfriars. He owns extensive property in Virginia and Bermuda.

The refugee's son has become a property owner in three countries.

---

What did Gideon think about all of this?

We do not know. He left no journal, no autobiography, no record of his inner life. We have only the facts of what he built, what he owned, what he achieved.

But we can imagine.

A boy who fled the St Bartholomew's Day Massacre at seven years old. A boy who watched his father hauled before the College of Physicians for practising without a licence. A boy who learned that standing - the recognition that lets you exist within a system rather than outside it - is everything.

And then a man who spent his life making sure his family would never again stand outside.

## NOW

I think about Gideon's properties.

Virginia. Bermuda.

The American colonies.

Gideon De Laune owned land in the New World at the same time the VOC was mapping New Holland. At the same time the First Fleet was being planned. At the same time the architecture that would become Australia was beginning to form.

Did he ever go there?

We do not know. The records show only that he owned property. Not what he did with it, or who managed it, or whether his family ever visited.

But this I know: the Faunce family - the family that would marry into the De Laune name and carry it to Australia - was in England at the same time Gideon was building his society.

Two threads. Running parallel. Not yet joined.

## THEN

The Society of Apothecaries still exists.

Four hundred and seven years after Gideon De Laune helped found it, the Society continues to grant licences and conduct examinations. It is the oldest surviving pharmaceutical regulatory body in the world.

Gideon's white marble statue still stands in Apothecaries' Hall.

The framework he built has outlasted empires.

---

In 1815 - the same year Mount Tambora erupts and creates the Year Without a Summer, the same year Britain consolidates its Pacific position after the Napoleonic Wars - the Apothecaries Act gives the Society statutory power to licence medical practitioners across England and Wales.

The framework Gideon helped design in 1617 is completed in 1815.

Two hundred years after his death.

The architecture of licensing that began with a refugee's son who fled a massacre has become the system that controls who can practice medicine in England.

NOT THE HAND became THE HAND.

And THE HAND never lets go.

## NOW

I sit at 3am in Cairns and I try to understand what this means for me.

My ancestor built the licensing framework for medicine in England.

Four hundred years later, I have no licensing framework for electronics in Australia.

The same bloodline. The same question of standing. The same architecture.

But on opposite sides.

Gideon built the framework. I navigate its absence.

Gideon became THE HAND. I am NOT THE HAND.

And yet.

The question that started on a fishing boat at Ashmore Reef - why is there no licence? - has led me to Gideon. To the Society of Apothecaries. To the moment in 1617 when a refugee's son transformed persecution into power.

The thread pulls and pulls.

And I follow it.

## THEN

In 1659, Gideon De Laune dies.

He is ninety-four years old. He has outlived his wife Judith Chamberlen, cousin of the man who invented obstetric forceps. He has outlived most of his seventeen children. He has seen the Civil War, the execution of Charles I, the rise and fall of the Commonwealth, the restoration of the monarchy.

He is buried under the stone in the porch of St Anne's Church, Blackfriars.

His statue stands in Apothecaries' Hall.

His pills continue to sell.

His properties pass to his surviving children.

And the framework he helped build - the licensing architecture that transforms skill into standing, that gives refugees a place within the system rather than outside it - continues to grow.

The Society of Apothecaries will not be the only framework Gideon's descendants help create.

But that is the next chapter.

I am still at 3am.

The screen still glows.

The thread is still pulling.

Gideon De Laune built the licensing framework for medicine.

His descendants - the Faunce family - will enforce the absence of frameworks in Australia.

The same bloodline. The same architecture. Different sides.

And I am the first one in four hundred years to name it.
