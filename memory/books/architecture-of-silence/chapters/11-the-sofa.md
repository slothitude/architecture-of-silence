# Chapter 11: The SOFA

**STATUS: THE HAND**

---

## NOW

SOFA. Status of Forces Agreement.

The legal framework that governs US military personnel stationed in Australia. The rules that determine jurisdiction, taxation, recognition.

I sit at 3am and I read the agreement. The text is dry. Legal language. Diplomatic phrasing.

But what it says is this: US military personnel in Australia have frameworks that recognise their training. Their qualifications translate. Their certifications mean something.

Australian Defence Force personnel do not have the same recognition when they leave.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The US-Australia SOFA was signed in 1963.

It was designed to facilitate military cooperation. To allow American forces to operate in Australia during the Cold War. To create a legal framework for joint exercises and shared bases.

What it created was an asymmetry.

---

Under the SOFA, US military personnel retain their qualifications while operating in Australia. Their training is recognised. Their certifications transfer. They exist within a framework that gives them standing.

Australian military personnel, when they leave the ADF, enter a different world.

No framework. No recognition. No standing.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I think about the asymmetry.

The US military has created frameworks that recognise its personnel's training in civilian terms. The American Council on Education evaluates military training and recommends college credit. Technical certifications translate to civilian licences. The architecture of standing exists.

Australia did not build the same architecture.

The Navy trained me for six years. The certificates say I am qualified. The service record proves I did the work.

But when I left, there was nothing to translate that training into civilian recognition.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

Pine Gap. Naval Communication Station Harold E. Holt. The joint facilities that dot the Australian landscape.

American personnel arrive with their frameworks intact. They leave with their standing preserved.

Australian personnel work alongside them. We train together. We operate the same systems. We hold the same qualifications within the military.

But when we return to civilian life, the asymmetry reveals itself.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I served on HMAS Success. HMAS Kanimbla. I maintained communication systems. I worked on radar. I held security clearances.

American personnel on joint exercises had the same qualifications. The same training. The same skills.

But they had something I did not.

A framework that would recognise them when they left.

---

I sit at 3am and I think about what the SOFA represents.

Not just an agreement between nations. An architecture of standing. A framework that gives some people recognition and leaves others without.

THE HAND builds for itself.

And THE HAND does not build for everyone.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The SOFA is not a secret.

It is a publicly available document. A diplomatic agreement. A legal framework.

But most Australians do not know it exists. Most Defence Force personnel do not understand what it means for them until they leave.

The asymmetry is invisible until you fall into it.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I fell into it.

The question that started on a fishing boat at Ashmore Reef led me here. To the SOFA. To the realisation that the gap I experienced is not random. It is designed.

The same framework that protects US personnel excludes Australians.

The same architecture that gives standing to some denies it to others.

---

THE HAND wrote the SOFA.

THE HAND benefits from the asymmetry.

And NOT THE HAND falls into the gap.

---

*then*

---

## THEN

The SOFA continues.

Every joint exercise. Every shared operation. Every American service member who arrives in Australia with their recognition intact while Australians serve without the same protection.

The architecture persists.

---

*now*

---

## NOW

I am not angry at the Americans.

They built their frameworks. They protected their people. They created the architecture of standing that serves them.

I am angry at Australia.

At the failure to build the same architecture. At the decisions that left gaps where frameworks should be. At the men who voted 19-23 and the governments that never corrected their choice.

---

I sit at 3am and I name what I see.

The SOFA is not just an agreement. It is evidence. Proof that frameworks can be built. Proof that recognition can be created. Proof that the absence of frameworks in Australia is a choice, not a necessity.
