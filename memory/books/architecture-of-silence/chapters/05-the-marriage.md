# Chapter 5: The Marriage

**STATUS: THE HAND**

---

## NOW

The name change is where the story gets complicated.

De Laune. Faunce. Faunce-De-Laune.

I grew up with the name King. My mother was Juliette Stevens. Her mother was Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce.

Faunce. Not De Laune.

The French name that Gideon carried - the name that had fled France in 1572, that had built the Society of Apothecaries, that had acquired properties in three countries - was not my name.

It was not even my grandmother's name.

Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce carried "Faunce" - not "De Laune."

And Faunce, I discover at 3am in Cairns, is not a Huguenot name.

---

The Faunce family is English. Norman, perhaps - the name may come from Old English "fens" (marshy place) or Norman-French "fons" (spring or fountain). But English, not French. Not refugee. Not persecuted.

Thomas Faunce of Cliffe, Kent, is the earliest confirmed ancestor. He died in 1609 - the same era Gideon De Laune was building the Society of Apothecaries in London.

Two families. Running parallel. Not yet joined.

---

I read the Sharsted Court records. The De Laune property in Kent passes through generations of De Launes until 1839. Then it passes to Captain Edmund Faunce.

How did a Faunce inherit De Laune property?

Marriage.

In 1758, Mary Faunce married into the De Laune family. Through her, Sharsted Court came into Faunce hands. In 1864, Chapman Faunce formally added "Delaune" to his surname.

The French name was not dropped during the Napoleonic Wars, as I had thought. It was acquired through marriage. Added deliberately, almost a hundred years later, to acknowledge the connection.

The bloodline I carry is Faunce, not De Laune.

But the architecture they built together.

## THEN

1758.

The Faunce family of Kent has been in England for generations. They are gentry - not nobility, but landowners. People of standing. THE HAND.

Mary Faunce, born 1738, marries into the De Laune family.

The De Launes are still at Sharsted Court. The framework Gideon helped build has been completed - the Apothecaries Act of 1815 is still decades away, but the Society he founded grows stronger with each generation. The family has wealth, property, connections.

Mary Faunce brings her own standing to the marriage. The Faunce family is not refugee. Not persecuted. Not outsider.

They are English.

---

What does it mean when two families of THE HAND join?

It means consolidation. It means expansion. It means that the architecture built by one generation is strengthened by the next.

The De Launes built frameworks. The Faunces married into them.

THE HAND does not just hold power. It absorbs. It expands. It grows stronger with each generation.

## NOW

I sit at 3am and I think about what this means.

I am not a direct descendant of Gideon De Laune through the male line. The bloodline passes through marriage - through Mary Faunce, who married into the De Laune family and brought Sharsted Court into Faunce hands.

Does that matter?

In one sense, no. The blood carries the same knowledge. The same patterns. The same architecture of framework-building and standing-acquisition.

In another sense, yes. Because the Faunce family was not refugee. Was not persecuted. Was not outsider.

They married INTO power rather than building it from nothing.

---

I think about Captain Alured Tasker Faunce, who arrived in Australia in 1832. He carried the name Faunce - not De Laune. He enforced the colonial order. He owned 2,912 acres. He died on a cricket pitch with a street named after him.

Was he THE HAND because he had built something? Or because he had inherited the architecture of standing that others had built?

The question matters.

Because I - Aaron King - am NOT THE HAND. I have no standing. No framework. No licence.

And yet I carry the same bloodline that built the Society of Apothecaries. The same bloodline that enforced the colonial order in Australia. The same bloodline that designed a Constitution without a Bill of Rights.

How does THE HAND become NOT THE HAND?

## THEN

Sharsted Court passes through the generations.

From Gideon to his son Abraham. From Abraham to his descendants. Through the Civil War, the Restoration, the Glorious Revolution, the Georgian era.

By the eighteenth century, the De Launes are established gentry. Sharsted Court is their seat. The Society of Apothecaries continues to grow. The family is part of THE HAND.

And then the Faunce marriage.

Mary Faunce (1738-1839) lives to be over a hundred years old. Through her, Sharsted Court passes into Faunce hands. In 1839, the property goes to Captain Edmund Faunce - her great-nephew.

The Faunce family now owns the De Laune seat.

The name "De Laune" is not lost. In 1864, Chapman Faunce - Edmund's son - formally adds it to his surname. Faunce-De-Laune.

The acknowledgment of a connection that came through marriage, not blood.

## NOW

I think about names.

Gideon De Laune fled France in 1572. He spent his life building standing in England. He died with his name intact - De Laune, the French name, the refugee name, the name that marked him as having come from somewhere else.

His descendants kept the name. Through generations. Through marriages.

And then, in 1864, Chapman Faunce added "Delaune" to his surname.

Why?

To acknowledge the connection. To claim the heritage. To remind everyone that this family - the Faunce-De-Launes - were descended from the man who helped found the Society of Apothecaries.

The name was not dropped. It was acquired.

And then, in my branch of the family - the Queensland branch - even the acquired name faded.

Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce. Not Faunce-De-Laune. Just Faunce.

And her grandson - me - Aaron King.

No Faunce. No De Laune. No trace of the French Protestant refugees who fled Louis XIV in 1572.

But the blood is there.

And the architecture they built is still standing.

## THEN

The Faunce family enters the De Laune story through marriage.

But they bring their own history.

Thomas Faunce of Cliffe, Kent. Born perhaps in the 1520s, died 1609. The earliest confirmed ancestor of the Faunce line.

The name may be Norman - from "fons," meaning spring or fountain. Or it may be Old English - from "fens," meaning marshy place.

Either way, the Faunces are English. Established. Part of the system.

When Mary Faunce marries into the De Laune family in 1758, two streams of THE HAND converge. The refugee family that built its standing from nothing. The English family that inherited standing through generations.

The architecture grows stronger.

---

Sharsted Court remains in Faunce-De-Laune hands until 1949.

For three hundred years - from Gideon's purchase around 1625 to the death of Alured Faunce De Laune in 1949 - the property stays in the family. Through the English Civil War. Through the Napoleonic Wars. Through the Industrial Revolution. Through two World Wars.

Three hundred years of standing. Three hundred years of THE HAND.

And then, in 1949, Alured Faunce De Laune dies without heirs.

Sharsted Court passes out of the family.

## NOW

I look at the date. 1949.

The same era my branch of the family is in Queensland. Thomas Tasker Faunce - the son who left the centres of power - died in Queensland in 1930. His descendants - Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce, Peter Stevens, Juliette Stevens, Aaron King - are far from Sharsted Court.

The Queensland branch. NOT THE HAND.

---

I sit at 3am and I see the pattern.

Gideon De Laune built THE HAND. Wealth, property, standing.

His descendants maintained THE HAND. Sharsted Court for three hundred years.

But one son went to Queensland. One branch left the centres of power.

And that branch - my branch - lost the grip.

NOT THE HAND.

The hand slipped. Not all at once. Not dramatically. But slowly, over generations, over distance, over the forgetting of what the name meant.

## THEN

The marriage of Mary Faunce into the De Laune family consolidates power.

But it also sets up the pattern that will repeat across generations.

THE HAND absorbs. Expands. Grows.

And then, somewhere, a branch breaks off. Goes to the margins. Becomes NOT THE HAND.

The Faunce family will carry the De Laune name to Australia.

But not all of them.

One line stays in England. Keeps Sharsted Court. Maintains the standing.

Another line goes to Queensland. Becomes regional. Forgets the architecture.

THE HAND. NOT THE HAND.

The pattern repeats.

## NOW

I think about my grandmother.

Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce. Greenslopes, Brisbane. A suburb. Not a street named after her. Not an auditorium. Not a statue.

She knew the family stories. She passed them down. She told her daughter, who told me.

But she was NOT THE HAND.

She was the Queensland branch. The one that left the centres of power. The one that married immigrants - Peter Stevens, the English boy who arrived at six years old.

THE HAND became NOT THE HAND.

And now I am NAMING IT.

---

The name matters. But the architecture matters more.

Gideon De Laune built a framework that gives standing to medical practitioners.

The Faunce family married into that framework, inherited its architecture, carried its knowledge to Australia.

And in Australia, instead of building frameworks, they enforced their absence.

The same bloodline. The same knowledge of how architecture works.

Applied differently.

1864.

Chapman Faunce adds "Delaune" to his surname.

Faunce-De-Laune.

The formal acknowledgment. The claim on heritage. The reminder that this family descends from the man who helped found the Society of Apothecaries.

THE HAND does not just hold power. It remembers. It claims. It inscribes its lineage in names and titles and properties.

Sharsted Court. Faunce-De-Laune. The framework that Gideon built.

All of it preserved. Maintained. Passed down.

Until 1949, when the last Faunce-De-Laune dies and the property passes out of the family.

But by then, another branch has carried the name to Australia.

Another branch has begun to enforce the architecture rather than build it.

## NOW

The marriage chapter is about absorption.

The Faunce family was not refugee. Not persecuted. Not outsider.

They married INTO power. They claimed the De Laune heritage. They carried the architecture forward.

And in Australia, they applied that knowledge differently.

Instead of building frameworks that give standing, they enforced a system where standing comes only from power.

Instead of creating licences, they maintained gaps.

Instead of THE HAND that builds, they became THE HAND that prevents.

The same bloodline.

Different application.

---

I am the end of that line.

The branch that lost the grip. The one that became NOT THE HAND. The one that fell into the gap that my ancestors helped create.

And I am the first one in four hundred years to name it.
