# The Architecture of Silence

## Metadata
- **Working Title**: The Architecture of Silence
- **Author**: Aaron King
- **Genre**: Literary Non-Fiction / Narrative History
- **Estimated Length**: 90,000 - 110,000 words
- **Thesis**: A family that fled persecution built the licensing frameworks of England, then enforced the colonial order in Australia, then found themselves inside the architecture they had created - until one of them finally named it.
- **Scope**: 1572 to present
- **Status**: First Draft Complete

---

## The Central Tension

**THE HAND** = Enforcing the system, serving power, inside the architecture
**NOT THE HAND** = Subject to the system, outside power, inside the architecture as victim

**The Rhythm:**

```

NOT THE HAND  →  no licence, hauled before College
BECOMING      →  building the framework

NOT THE HAND  →  silenced by what she saw
NOT THE HAND  →  immigrant, subject to the system

```

# BOOK ONE: NOT THE HAND
*The family before they had standing*

---

## Chapter 1: The Massacre (1572)

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND**

**NOW**: Ashmore Reef, Timor Sea, mid-1990s. Aaron King, twenty years old, steps aboard an Indonesian fishing vessel. A dead man among hundreds of trepang. A map showing only the northern coast of Australia - the part that existed before Australia.

**THEN**: France, August 1572. Gideon De Laune, age seven, watches his father William gather what can be carried. Outside, the killing has begun. St Bartholomew's Day. Three thousand Protestants murdered in Paris in one night. Seventy thousand across France. Men sent to the galleys. Women locked up. Children taken to convents. The family flees to the coast with nothing.

**THE RHYTHM**: This is what it looks like when you have no standing. When the state removes the frameworks that protect you. When you are NOT THE HAND.

**CLOSING**: "Lieutenant George Faunce noted a small boat in his journal and went to dinner. I am not going to dinner."

---

## Chapter 2: The Licence (1582)

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND**

**NOW**: HMAS Benalla, 2015. Both hands on a live antenna. Wrong standard. Wrong distance. Six years in the Navy. Certificate III and IV. The question begins: why can't I fix a television? No Electronics Technician licence exists in Australia. training.gov.au - no licensing requirements. The circular trap.

**THEN**: London, 1582. William De Laune, French physician and Protestant minister, is hauled before the College of Physicians. Eight years of medical training in Paris and Montpellier. A degree from Europe's premier medical school. No standing in England. No English licence. The original licence problem - four hundred and thirty-three years before Aaron King.

**THE RHYTHM**: Father and descendant, same problem, different centuries. Neither of them THE HAND. Neither of them with standing. Both of them facing the same architecture from the outside.

**THE PARALLEL**:
| William De Laune (1582) | Aaron King (2015) |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| Eight years training | Six years training |
| No English licence | No Australian licence |
| College of Physicians case | No regulatory framework exists |
| NOT THE HAND | NOT THE HAND |

# BOOK TWO: BECOMING THE HAND
*The framework is built*

---

## Chapter 3: The Builder (1617)

**STATUS: BECOMING THE HAND**

**NOW**: Aaron researching at 3am. Finding the Society of Apothecaries. Finding Gideon De Laune. Finding Francis Bacon. The year 1617. The Rosicrucian manifestos circulating across Europe. The charter granted. The licensing framework being built.

**THEN**: London, 1617. Gideon De Laune - the seven-year-old boy who fled the massacre - is now apothecary to Queen Anne of Denmark. He works with Francis Bacon and Sir Theodore de Mayerne to plan the Society of Apothecaries. The charter is granted by King James I on 6 December 1617. The licensing framework for medicine in England is born.

**THE RHYTHM**: This is the moment of transformation. The family that had NO STANDING builds the framework that GIVES standing. NOT THE HAND becomes THE HAND. The persecuted become the licencers.

**KEY LINE**: "Your ancestor spent his life creating the licensing framework. You spent your life navigating its absence."

---

## Chapter 4: The Singularities (1659)

**STATUS: THE HAND**

**NOW**: Aaron finding the phrase. "Noted for many singularities in his time." What did it mean? Distinctive, unusual, remarkable. The phrase that points to something not quite ordinary. The white marble statue still standing in Blackfriars. The family has arrived.

**THEN**: London, 1659. Gideon De Laune dies at age 94. He leaves an estate worth £93,000 - mostly from "De Laune's Pills," a secret remedy for scurvy, jaundice, venereal disease, worms, dropsy. His properties: Roxton Bedfordshire, Sharsted Kent, Blackfriars mansion, Virginia, Bermuda. His statue stands in Apothecaries' Hall. The refugee with nothing has become THE HAND.

**THE RHYTHM**: The hand is now formed. Wealth. Property in three countries. A statue. A secret formula that made a fortune. The family that fled persecution has become the establishment.

# BOOK THREE: THE HAND
*Enforcing the architecture*

---

## Chapter 5: The Marriage (1758)

**STATUS: THE HAND**

**NOW**: Aaron finding the name. De Laune becomes Faunce. Not through blood - through marriage. The Faunce family of Kent. Thomas Faunce at Cliffe. They marry into the De Laune power. The hand acquires new fingers.

**THEN**: Kent, 1758. Mary Faunce (1738-1839) marries into the De Laune family. Sharsted Court passes to the Faunce family through her. In 1864, Chapman Faunce adds "Delaune" to his surname. The formal acknowledgment. The hand consolidating its grip.

**THE RHYTHM**: THE HAND doesn't just hold power - it expands. It absorbs. The Faunce family was not Huguenot, not refugee, not persecuted. They were English gentry. They married INTO the De Laune power. THE HAND grew stronger.

---

## Chapter 6: The Soldier (1759)

**STATUS: THE HAND**

**NOW**: Aaron finding the military line. Major Thomas Faunce at Quebec 1759. Major-General Alured Dodsworth Faunce in the Napoleonic Wars. The family that fled persecution became the family that fought for the Crown that took them in.

**THEN**: Quebec, 1759. Major Thomas Faunce serves under General Wolfe at the Battle of the Plains of Abraham. Wounded. Becomes town major of Quebec when it is occupied. The hand holds the sword now. The persecuted have become the conquerors.

**THE RHYTHM**: THE HAND doesn't just serve the system - it expands it. Quebec. Empire. The family that fled the massacre now helps build the British Empire.

## Chapter 7: The Convicts (1788)

**STATUS: THE HAND (THE LIE)**

**NOW**: Aaron finding the First Fleet records. The Lady Penrhyn - already contracted for China tea before convicts boarded. The Russian hemp crisis. Britain needed hemp. Catherine restricted supply. The prison story told to bypass EIC monopoly fees and avoid European rivals contesting the Pacific base. Half the convicts said no occupation. Protecting their labour.

**THEN**: 1788. The First Fleet arrives. The official story: a humanitarian solution to overcrowded gaols. The reality: a Pacific strategic base. Hemp. Tea. Position against European rivals. The convicts were the cover story. The oversell began with the first ship.

**THE RHYTHM**: THE HAND writes the official story. "Convict colony." "Nothing here." "Prison solution." The same language the Navy recruiting officer used with Aaron - "contemporary and transferable." The same language Cook used about Botany Bay. The same language the VOC used about New Holland. THE HAND doesn't just enforce - it NARRATES.

**KEY LINE**: "The convicts were never the point. The convicts were the excuse. The point was position."

---

## Chapter 8: The Magistrate (1832-1856)

**STATUS: THE HAND**

**NOW**: Aaron finding Captain Faunce in Australia. "Ironman Faunce." Police Magistrate at Brisbane Water, then Queanbeyan. The Blindberry case. £1,500 damages. Forced to sell his commission. Then rebuilt. 2,912 acres. Died on a cricket pitch. Faunce Street in O'Connor ACT.

**THEN**: Sydney, 1832. Captain Alured Tasker Faunce arrives with the 4th Regiment. He is 24 years old. He will spend the next 24 years in New South Wales. He will enforce the colonial order over people who were promised land and did not receive it. The hand writes the rules. The hand carries the gun. The hand signs the papers.

**THE RHYTHM**: THE HAND at its most direct. Not just serving power - ENFORCING it. Magistrate. Land owner. The system Captain Faunce enforced was the same architecture his ancestor Gideon had helped build in 1617. Licences. Standing. Who has it. Who doesn't.

**KEY LINE**: "The family that built the licensing framework in England enforced the absence of frameworks in Australia."

---

## Chapter 9: The Constitution (1898)

**STATUS: THE HAND**

**NOW**: Aaron finding the Bill of Rights removal. 1898. Colonial chief justices and financial interests go to London. The squatters' heirs design a country where Parliament gives rights rather than a document that constrains Parliament. The hand without a thumb.

**THEN**: 1898. Rev. Canon Alured Dodsworth Faunce - son of Captain Faunce - serves as Anglican rector in the Bega and Yass diocese. He watches Federation being built. The Bill of Rights is removed. The Constitution written without a thumb. THE HAND shapes the architecture that will hold for 125 years.

**THE VOTE**: Bill of Rights put to convention: **19 FOR, 23 AGAINST**.

**THE RHYTHM**: THE HAND doesn't just enforce - it DESIGNS. The Constitution without a Bill of Rights. The hand that signed the magistrate's papers now helps shape the nation's foundation. Not by what was built. By what was never built.

## Chapter 9B: The Conspiracy (1891-1988)

**STATUS: THE HAND (THE PATTERN)**

**NOW**: Aaron finding the connections. The 1891 shearers' strike. The 1898 vote. The 1946 UKUSA Agreement. The 1966 Pine Gap base. The 1975 dismissal. The 1988 referendum. The same interests. The same architecture. The same silence.

**THEN**: The conspiracy across a century.

**1891 - THE PRECEDENT**:
- Shearers' strike - military deployed to protect non-union labour
- Strike leaders arrested for "sedition" - 3 years prison
- Samuel Griffith (constitutional author) personally praised prosecution
- Pattern established: Government + Squatters = force against workers

**1898 - THE VOTE**:
- Bill of Rights voted down 19-23
- Squatter interests protected:
  - Property rights challenges from Indigenous peoples
  - Workers' organising rights (threatened cheap labour)
  - Equal protection (would undermine discriminatory laws)

**1946-1966 - THE EMBEDDING**:
- UKUSA Agreement (1946) - Australia secretly joins 1956
- Pine Gap established 1966 - CIA/NSA base near Alice Springs
- Prime Minister not told of Five Eyes until 1973
- No Bill of Rights = no constitutional challenge to mass surveillance

**1975 - THE WARNING**:
- Whitlam threatened to close Pine Gap
- Dismissed the same day he was to brief Parliament on CIA presence
- CIA whistleblower: Kerr called "**our man Kerr**"
- Message: Challenge intelligence apparatus = removed from power

**1988 - THE FAILURE**:
- Rights referendum crushed 69-31%
- Churches mobilised against (feared church-state separation)
- No political support
- Intelligence services avoid constitutional constraints

**THE PATTERN**:

| What's Missing | Who Benefits |
|----------------|--------------|
| No Bill of Rights | Intelligence (unimpeded surveillance) |
| No ET licence | Five Eyes (US technicians invisible to Australian law) |
| No press freedom | Government (journalists can be jailed) |
| No whistleblower protection | Intelligence (no leaks) |
| No Indigenous treaty | Corporate (no land challenges) |

**THE RHYTHM**: THE HAND doesn't just design - it PROTECTS. The same interests that opposed workers in 1891, embedded spy bases in 1966, removed a Prime Minister in 1975, defeated rights in 1988. Every missing framework serves the same masters. The architecture is not random. It is designed.

**KEY LINE**: "Australia is the only western democracy without a Bill of Rights. The only Five Eyes country without an ET licence. The only Five Eyes country where journalists can be raided. These absences cluster around one thing - the ability of ordinary Australians to question what is being done to them and around them. And they have been maintained continuously across every government since Federation."

## Chapter 10: The Physician (1975)

**STATUS: THE HAND**

**NOW**: Aaron finding Marcus de Laune Faunce. Personal physician to Gough Whitlam and five other Prime Ministers. Six Governors-General. The Faunce Auditorium at Royal Canberra Hospital. A Faunce present at the moment the correction is prevented.

**THEN**: 11 November 1975. Marcus de Laune Faunce in Canberra. Whitlam's physician. The morning Kerr summons Whitlam. The names that were never said. The reserve powers - deliberately unwritten in 1898 - used for the first time. THE HAND present at the moment the architecture holds.

**THE RHYTHM**: THE HAND doesn't just design and enforce - it WITNESSES. Marcus de Laune Faunce was not the enforcer like Captain Faunce. He was not the designer like the constitutional delegates. He was the physician. Present. Serving. THE HAND inside the room when the correction was prevented.

**WHAT WHITLAM WAS GOING TO NAME**:

The official story says Whitlam was going to name CIA operatives. But there's more.

Whitlam was going to expose the **Electronics Technician gap**. He was going to ask: why does Australia have no ET licence? Why are 1,700 US Electronics Technicians operating at Pine Gap and other facilities under SOFA with no Australian regulatory oversight? Why is the only Five Eyes country that hosts the joint facilities the only one without a licence for the technicians who run them?

If Whitlam had named it, the entire SOFA arrangement would have been exposed. The architecture would have been named in 1975. Instead, the dismissal happened. The names were never said. The gap remained.

**Aaron King would not exist as a story without 11 November 1975.** The gap that was never named became the gap Aaron fell into forty years later.

**KEY LINE**: "Captain Faunce enforced the system. Marcus de Laune Faunce served its leaders. Aaron King would be the first Faunce on the wrong side of it - and the one who finally named what Whitlam was prevented from naming."

## Chapter 10: The Queensland Line (1847)

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND (THE BEGINNING)**

**NOW**: Aaron finding Thomas Tasker Faunce. Born 15 February 1847. Died 9 November 1930. Died in Queensland. The line that left the centres of power. The line that went north. The line where THE HAND began to lose its grip.

**THEN**: Queanbeyan, 1847. Thomas Tasker Faunce is born - the seventh child of Captain Alured Tasker Faunce. He will not follow his father into the magistrate's office. He will not follow his brother Alured into the church. He will go to Queensland. The branch that will become NOT THE HAND.

**THE RHYTHM**: THE HAND begins to slip. Not dramatically. Not all at once. But one son goes north. One line leaves the centres of power. The Queensland branch will not have Faunce Street named after them. They will not have auditoriums. They will not serve prime ministers.

## Chapter 11: The Scottish Nurse (WW2)

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND**

**NOW**: Aaron asking his mother Juliette what she remembers. The nurse. The ship. Near Brisbane during WW2. What she saw. Why she drank. The story sitting in the family for 80 years. The silence that passes through blood.

**THEN**: World War 2. The Scottish nurse - Aaron's great-grandmother - on a ship near Brisbane. The Timor Sea. The same ocean. What she sees. The thing that cannot be unfiled. The drinking that follows. NOT THE HAND - silenced by what she witnessed.

**THE RHYTHM**: NOT THE HAND doesn't just lack power - it is CRUSHED by power. The Scottish nurse saw something. Something that broke her. She carried the silence for the rest of her life. NOT THE HAND doesn't enforce. It suffers.

## Chapter 12: The Boy (1930s)

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND**

**NOW**: Peter Stevens. Aaron's grandfather. Six years old when he emigrated from England to Brisbane. Grew up in Brisbane during the war. MacArthur on Queen Street. Battle of Brisbane suppressed. Brisbane Line controversy. The Architecture of Silence road tested on his doorstep.

**THEN**: Late 1930s. Peter Stevens, six years old, standing on the deck of a ship. England disappearing behind him. The ocean ahead. Not knowing he will never go back. Not knowing he is entering the architecture his wife's ancestors built.

**THE RHYTHM**: NOT THE HAND doesn't know it's NOT THE HAND. Peter Stevens arrived as an immigrant. He became a doctor. He married Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce. He didn't know the Faunce name had once been THE HAND. He just lived inside the architecture.

## Chapter 13: Elizabeth Yvonne (1900s)

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND**

**NOW**: Aaron finding his grandmother. Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce. Greenslopes, Brisbane. The Queensland branch. The name "Faunce" but not the power. "De Laune" dropped - or never really acquired, because the Faunce family married into it and the Queensland branch married out of the centres where it mattered.

**THEN**: Greenslopes, Brisbane. Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce grows up. Her mother is the Scottish nurse who saw something and never recovered. She marries Peter Stevens, the English boy who arrived at six. She has a daughter, Juliette. She knows the family stories. But she is NOT THE HAND.

**THE RHYTHM**: NOT THE HAND carries the memory but not the power. Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce knew the stories. She passed them down. But she lived in Greenslopes, not Canberra. She was the branch that let go of the grip.

*The first to write it down*

## Chapter 14: The Five Missing Frameworks

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND / BECOMING NAMING IT**

**NOW**: Aaron building the complete list. No Bill of Rights. No ET licence. No press freedom protection. No whistleblower protection. No Indigenous treaty. 875 secrecy offences. Zero constitutional rights to speak. Every other Five Eyes country has all of them. Australia uniquely does not.

**THEN**: The pattern emerges. 1572 - frameworks removed, persecution follows. 1582 - no licence, no standing. 1617 - framework built. 1832-1856 - enforcing the absence of frameworks. 1898 - framework deliberately not built. 1963 - SOFA ensures framework never built for ETs. 2015 - no ET licence, no standing.

**THE RHYTHM**: The question crystallises. NOT THE HAND is not an accident. The absence of frameworks is not random. The architecture was built. Deliberately. Over four centuries. And Aaron - NOT THE HAND - is beginning to see it.

## Chapter 15: The Live Antenna (2015)

**STATUS: NOT THE HAND**

**NOW**: HMAS Benalla. Both hands on a live antenna. Wrong standard. Wrong distance. The silence after. The question that will not stop. Why is there no licence? Why is there no framework? Why am I the only Five Eyes technician with no standing?

**THEN**: The architecture holds. The system that Captain Faunce enforced, that the 1898 convention designed, that the SOFA maintained - it works exactly as intended. Aaron has no standing. No licence. No framework to give him standing. NOT THE HAND.

**THE RHYTHM**: NOT THE HAND feels the architecture from inside. Not as a historian reading about it. Not as a theorist analysing it. As a technician with both hands on a live antenna. The body knows what the mind hasn't articulated yet.

## Chapter 16: Three Times From Nothing

**STATUS: NAMING IT**

**NOW**: Aaron in Cairns. Five years sober. Three careers built from deficits. Navy ET to restricted electrical licence to Chief Engineer MED1 to independent programmer. The man who rebuilds because that is what you do. Teaching himself to code at 3am. Pulling the thread.

**THEN**: Captain Faunce after the Blindberry case. Forced to sell his commission. Pays £1,500 damages. Rebuilds. Becomes Senior Magistrate anyway. The Faunce template - you get knocked down, you get back up, you keep going. Aaron inherited this. But he used it differently.

**THE RHYTHM**: THE HAND rebuilt to keep enforcing. NOT THE HAND rebuilt to name the architecture. Same template. Different purpose. Captain Faunce rebuilt to continue serving power. Aaron rebuilt to understand why he had to rebuild at all.

---

## Chapter 17: The Question Answered

**STATUS: NAMING IT**

**NOW**: 3am in Cairns. Aaron has followed the thread all the way back. From the trepang boat to the massacre in France. From the live antenna to the original licence problem in 1582. From the missing framework to the man who built the framework. From NOT THE HAND to THE HAND to NOT THE HAND to NAMING IT.

**THEN**: The complete arc. 450 years. A family that fled persecution built the licensing frameworks of England. Enforced the colonial order in Australia. Designed a Constitution without rights. Witnessed the dismissal that prevented correction. Then lost the grip. Went to Queensland. Married immigrants. Became NOT THE HAND. And finally - the first in 450 years - wrote it down.

**THE RHYTHM**: NAMING IT is not enforcing. NAMING IT is not suffering. NAMING IT is the third position. Not inside power, not crushed by power, but SEEING power. Articulating it. The Architecture of Silence named by the bloodline that built it, enforced it, lost it, and finally understood it.

# EPILOGUE: Paid in the Light

**STATUS: THE CIRCLE**

The song. Every step every breath I see - is the universe saying thank you through me.

Three reinventions. Five years sober. The trepang boat. Amsterdam. The antenna. Captain Faunce. The Scottish nurse. Peter Stevens. Elizabeth Yvonne Faunce.

The country belongs to us. That is what Ned Kelly said. His words were changed to *Such is life*. This book changes them back.

**Final flash**: Peter Stevens. Six years old. Standing on the deck of a ship. England disappearing. The ocean ahead. A child who does not know yet that he will never go back. That his grandson will stand on a trepang boat and refuse to go to dinner. That his bloodline - which built the architecture, enforced the architecture, lost the architecture, suffered the architecture - will finally be the one to NAME the architecture.

NOT THE HAND enforcing.
NOT THE HAND suffering.
NAMING IT.
